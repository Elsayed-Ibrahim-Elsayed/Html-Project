History of internet of things

Entrepreneur Kevin Ashton, one of the founders of the MIT Auto-ID Centre, coined the word "Internet of Things." Ashton was part of a team that discovered how an RFID tag could link objects to the internet. In a 1999 lecture, he used the term "Internet of Things" for the first time – and it has since stuck around.

Ashton may have been first to use the term Internet of Things, but the concept of connected devices – particularly connected machines – has been around for a long time. For example, machines have been communicating with each other since the first electric telegraphs were developed in the late 1830s.

Other technologies that fed into IoT were radio voice transmissions, wireless (Wi-Fi) technologies and supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) software. Then in 1982, a modified Coke machine at Carnegie Mellon University became the first connected smart appliance.
